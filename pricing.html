Frequently asked questions

What counts as one episode?

One episode equals one full AI production run: guest research, outreach email draft, interview question set, and show notes — all generated for a single guest and episode. Each run counts as one toward your monthly limit.

Can I upgrade or downgrade my plan?

Yes. You can change your plan at any time. Upgrades take effect immediately and are prorated. Downgrades apply at the start of your next billing cycle. No penalties or lock-in.

Is there a free trial?

PodPilot is currently in private beta. Request a demo and we'll walk you through the full product — no credit card needed. Early access members also receive a discounted launch price when we go live.

How does the Notion delivery work?

On the Studio plan, you connect your Notion workspace during onboarding. PodPilot creates a structured database in your workspace. Every time a new episode brief is generated, a new entry is automatically added with all the AI-produced content — guest profile, questions, outreach draft, and show notes.

How does PodPilot learn my show's niche?

When you set up your podcast profile, you tell PodPilot about your show — the topic, format, audience, and the type of guests you want to feature. PodPilot uses this context every time it runs a production brief, so every suggestion is tailored to your specific show — not a generic template.

Is my data secure?

Yes. PodPilot is built with enterprise-grade security from day one. All data is encrypted in transit and at rest. Enterprise plans include full ISO 27001, SOC 2, and GDPR compliance documentation. We never use your show data to train our models.
